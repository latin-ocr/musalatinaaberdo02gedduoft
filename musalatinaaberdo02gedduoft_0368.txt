272 ENCOMIA URBIUM.

14. FARFARA.

Farfara, te decorant regalis rudera tecti,
Et lacus, et multae fertilitatis ager.
Sunt angusta tibi, fateor, pomoeria, latum
Sed tamen imperium Scotia prisca dedit.
Quos geris, agnoscit tellus Angusia fasces,
Seque tuo sistunt rura remota foro.
Ius dicunt populi proceres, plebs excolit artem
Qua parat et tenues iugiter auget opes.
Tergora sunt illi validis detracta iuvencis,
Et quae virgineos velat aluta pedes, 10
Umbrorum levibus dedit haec sandalia plantis,
Et soleas sociis, funiger ordo, tuis.
Ne nive vel glacie laedaris tempore brumae,
Haec tibi perones, rustica turba, parat.
Fortibus haec ocreis veteres instruxit Achivos,
Et Graiae crepidas hinc petiere nurus.-
Plebs eadem tragicis munivit crura cothurnis,
Huius et inventurn nobile soccus erat.
Roma suas posthac ne tollat in aethera vires,
Nec bellatrices Sparta superba manus. 20
Imposuere iugum populi cervicibus illae,
Fortibus haec vinclis crura pedesque premit.

1. Regalis] Allusion to Malcolm Canmore’s residence at Forfar.
11. Umbrorum.] J. B. translates this distich thus :—

“ From thee are sandals to light Umbrians sent,
And solls with latchets to Rope-climbers lent ''.

The probability is that it is a cornie exaggeration, claiming that the subjects of the Pope in
Umbria and the Cordeliers or order of St. Francis got their sandals from Forfar. In the old
Statistical Account of Sc.‚ VI. 513, mention is made of the shoemaking of Forsar as still
(1796 or so) famous for a special fabric of shoes much prized “ in the Braes of Angus ”.

12. Funiger.] Probably a coinage of Johnston.

15. Achivos.] Allusion to the ‘¹ well-greaved ” Achaeans of the Iliad.
