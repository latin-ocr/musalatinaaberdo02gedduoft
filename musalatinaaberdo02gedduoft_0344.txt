CLXI. DEDICATION TO WM. LAUD, BISHOP OF LONDON.

This was prefixed to the issue of Johnston's version of the Seven Penitential Psalms (viz.,
Psalms 6, 32, 38, 51, 102, 130 and 143), published as an avant-comior volume in London, 1633.

ARGUMENT.

Thy piety‚ O Laud, bright with Divine intelligence, gives thee access to
the royal ear. Hence I dedicate to thee these Tears of the Hebrew Bard,
sure token of my loyalty. More I cannot give: the waters of Helicon are all
the reward the Muses can command. But if thou regard them with beams of
favour, the waters will turn into pure nectar by thy smile.

CLXI. AD REVERENDUM ANTISTITEM, GULIELMUM
LADIUM‚ EPISCOPUM LONDINENSEM‚ DEDICATIO.

Cui pietas, Ladi, caelestis acumine fulgens
Ingenii, Patriae dat Patris aure frui,
Has ego Iessaei lacrimas tibi nuncupo vatis:
Hae sint obsequii tessera certa mei.
Plus dare fas non est3 Helicon, quos nutrit, alumnis
Nil solet infelix fundere praeter aquas.
Ut te digna feras, adspira mollibus auris
Tu mihi, sic puro nectare vena fluet.
