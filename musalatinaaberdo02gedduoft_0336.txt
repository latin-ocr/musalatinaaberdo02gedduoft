3. THE KING To THE QUEENE.

While a short stay, short way divides us twaine,
Nor yet of me, nor of your hap complaine,

My Charles and Mary while you doe me misse
A thousand times your Rosy cheeke will kisse ι
And if our Physicks learned leach say true,

3. REX REGINAE.

Dum breve nos tempus, brevis et via dividit, uxor,
De me deque tua desine sorte queri.

Mille tibi pro me libabunt oscula nati
Carolus et fratri iuncta tenella soror:

Et, nisi me noster fallit Podalirius, intus
Quod te soletur pignus amoris habes.

4. THE QUEENES COMPLAINT OF THE SCOTS.

Thou warlike nation whom Achaius tyed

In league, and made our house of Borbones guard,
Since thou my love and mee dost now divide,
Thou art become my foe, whose heart so hard
T'enjoy my heart my owne life hast not spar'd.
My dayes want light, my nights sleep ; longer are
The houres then dayes, the yeere then age by farre ;
Either confesse our league is broken here,

Or Scotland quickly send me backe my deere.

4. REGINAE QUERIMONIA DE SCOTIS.

Borboniae tutela domus (gens Martia), nostris
Foedere quam prisco iunxit Achaius avis,

Dum te noster amor sine me petit, hostica facta es,
Meque, meo dum tu vivis amore, necas.
