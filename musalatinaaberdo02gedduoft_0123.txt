ARGUMENT.

Here you behold Vulturnus’s garden: he is rich, and his enclosure is
not to be traversed by mortal foot. Pomona and Flora there unfold all
‘their charms, and walls and sky-compearing crags protect the treasureS.
The Palladium and the Vesta] fire were not more watched; neither the
labyrinth nor Hypermnestra's prison was so secure, nor Erichthonius's
coffer, nor Danae‘s brazen tower. Others are masters of their property3 to
your garden you are the caretaker and the slave.

LXXX. DE HORTO VULTURNI.

Divitis hic, hospes, Vulturni conspicis hortum,
Septaque mortali non superanda pede.

Delicias Pomona suas hic explicat omnes,
Floraque purpureos pandit amoena sinus.

Ardua murorum moles, et proxima caelo
Has illibatas saxa tuentur opes.

Non ita Palladium custodivere Quirites,
Nobile Vestalis nee penetrale foci.

Non ita semivirum quae sepsit machina monstrum,
Carcer Hypermnestrae non ita tutus erat. 10

Sed nec Erichthonium sic clausit cista Minervae,
Nec dominam pluvii turris ahena Iovis.

Sunt alii rerum domini, Vulturne, suarum,
Tu custos horti mancipiumque tui es.
